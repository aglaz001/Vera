What Vera does
Vera is an ESG management and reporting platform. Companies come to Vera to collect their Environmental, Social, and Governance data, check compliance against frameworks like GRI and CSRD, and generate a publication-ready ESG report — without needing a sustainability consultant or a dedicated team. The target users are SMEs and Africa (Angola as the priority market). The core promise: a company with no prior ESG experience can produce a credible, compliant report in under 4 hours.
________________


Name & Brand Direction
Vera — Latin for "truth". An ESG report is fundamentally about telling the truth about your company's impact. The name is short, cross-cultural, works in Portuguese, French, English and Spanish, and has a quiet confidence to it. It doesn't scream "green tech" — it feels trustworthy and human.
________________


Visual Identity
Aesthetic direction: Ecologi-inspired — organic, editorial, calm authority. Not a typical SaaS product with gradients and glowing cards. Vera should feel like a high-end sustainability consultancy that happens to be a software product. Think natural materials, craft, intentionality.
Color palette:
* Primary — Forest #1A3D22 — deep, almost-black green. Used for headings, primary buttons, nav background option, footer.
* Secondary — Grove #2E6B40 — mid-range green for interactive elements, active states, icons.
* Accent — Fern #5AAF72 — brighter green for highlights, progress bars, badges, data accents.
* Light — Mist #EAF5ED — very pale green for card backgrounds, tag backgrounds, subtle section fills.
* Warm — Linen #F7F3EC — off-white cream for page background. Never pure white — Vera always has warmth.
* Text — Bark #0F2014 — near-black for body text and headings.
* Muted — Moss #4A6B52 — for secondary text, labels, captions.
* Subtle — Lichen #8FAF96 — for placeholder text, dividers, tertiary info.
No purple. No blue. No gradients. No dark mode in the marketing site — the warmth of linen and forest green is the identity.
________________


Typography
Display / Headings: Instrument Serif — elegant, editorial, with a beautiful italic. Used for all H1, H2, and any large statement text. The italic variant is used for emphasis within headlines (like Ecologi does — "hours, not months"). This is what gives Vera its personality.
Body / UI: DM Sans — clean, geometric, highly legible at small sizes. Weight 300 for long-form text, 400 for UI body, 500 for labels and buttons. Never 600 or 700 — heaviness breaks the organic feel.
Scale:
* Hero H1: 3.2–3.8rem, Instrument Serif, weight 400
* Section H2: 2.4–2.8rem, Instrument Serif, weight 400
* Card titles: 1rem, DM Sans, weight 500
* Body: 1rem / 1.05rem, DM Sans, weight 300–400, line-height 1.7
* Labels / tags: 0.75–0.8rem, DM Sans, weight 500, letter-spacing 0.08em
* Captions: 0.72rem, DM Sans, weight 400, color Moss
Italic rule: Instrument Serif italic is used inside headlines to give emotional emphasis — not in body text, not in UI. Reserve it for moments that matter.

Pages
3. ESG Dashboard The home screen after login. A circular score ring (0–100) at the top left, pillar breakdown bars (E / S / G) alongside it. Below: a 3-column metric grid (CO₂ total, turnover rate, board diversity, etc.). A compliance progress card per framework. A year-over-year trend chart in Fern green. A "top 3 improvement actions" block bottom right. Background Linen, cards in white with 1px Mist border.
4. Data entry — Environmental Tabbed interface: E / S / G across the top. Each tab is a structured form. Fields have inline unit labels, help tooltips, and a "why this matters" expandable note. Below each field cluster, a calculated result card appears in Mist background — "→ Scope 2 calculated: 180t CO₂e". File upload zone at the bottom for Excel/PDF parsing. Completion progress bar per section.
5. Data entry — Social Same layout as Environmental. HR metrics: headcount, turnover, injuries, training hours, gender pay gap. Some fields can be filled by inviting an HR team member — a "Assign to HR" chip on those fields. Calculated outputs: LTIFR, turnover rate %.
6. Data entry — Governance Same layout. Board composition, policies, ethics, whistleblower, data breaches. More boolean yes/no fields than the other two pillars. Policy document upload supported.
7. Compliance checker Full-page view of all framework disclosures as a list. Three status indicators: green dot (complete), amber dot (partial), red dot (missing). Clicking a row expands to show what's missing, why it's required, and a direct link to the relevant form field. Export gap report as PDF. Framework toggle at the top (GRI / CSRD / TCFD).
8. AI narrative editor Split view: left panel shows the data submitted for a section, right panel shows the AI-generated narrative paragraph. Edit button opens the text inline. Approve button marks it as reviewed. Version history accessible. Badge on each paragraph: "AI generated" until approved, then "Reviewed". Language selector at the top for multilingual output.
9. Report preview & export Full-page report preview rendered as it will appear in PDF. Sidebar with export options: PDF (branded), DOCX (editable), shareable link. Branding settings: upload logo, set accent color. A GRI Content Index appendix is generated automatically at the bottom. Download triggers a Celery background job — user gets a notification when ready.
10. Settings Tabs: Organization profile / Team members / Billing / Integrations / Notifications. Team member table with role badges (Admin / Editor / Viewer). Invite by email. Integration cards for ERP connectors (locked until Phase 2). Billing shows current plan, usage, and upgrade path.
11. Consultant portal (Phase 2) A special view for consultants. Left sidebar lists all client organizations. Clicking one enters that org's workspace. Top bar always shows which client you're viewing. Bulk actions: export all client reports, compare scores across clients.

Design Patterns
Cards: White background, 1px border in Mist (rgba(10,31,15,0.08)), border-radius: 14px, padding 1.4–1.6rem. No drop shadow on marketing cards. Subtle box-shadow: 0 4px 24px rgba(10,31,15,0.05) on dashboard cards only.
Buttons:
* Primary: Forest background, white text, border-radius: 100px (pill shape), DM Sans 500
* Ghost: transparent, Forest border 1.5px, Forest text, pill shape
* Danger: only for delete actions, subtle red
Tags / pills: Mist background, Fern text, very small font, pill shape. Used for framework labels, status indicators, field requirements.
Progress bars: 5–6px height, Fern fill on Mist background, fully rounded.
Score ring: SVG circle with stroke-dasharray animation. Forest color at high scores, amber at mid, red at low.
Forms: Fields have a warm-white background, 1px Lichen border, subtle focus ring in Fern. Labels in Moss, 0.78rem. No floating labels — always above the field.
Spacing rhythm: 8px base unit. Sections breathe with 5–6rem vertical padding. Content max-width 1200px, always centered.

Tone of voice
Vera speaks like a knowledgeable colleague, not a software manual. Direct, clear, never condescending. Encourages progress without pressure. When something is missing it says "3 fields needed to complete this section" — not "ERROR: incomplete data". The brand tagline is something like "ESG reporting for companies that actually make things."
